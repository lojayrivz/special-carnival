02
Tokyo — Pokémon Day 🎮
Tsukiji + Akihabara + PokéPark KANTO
Markets · Anime · The World's First Pokémon Park
⚠️ PokéPark KANTO tickets sell out fast — similar to Ghibli Park. Book via the international tourist portal at pokepark-kanto.co.jp as soon as tickets open for your dates (~2 months in advance). Two types: Trainer's Pass (~¥7,900) or Ace Trainer's Pass (~¥14,000) — both include Yomiuriland admission. Also download the official PokéPark KANTO app before arrival to grab your Daisuki Shop numbered ticket the moment you enter.
07:00
Food
Tsukiji Outer Market — Breakfast
Go EARLY (before 09:00) — tuna sashimi, tamago-yaki, grilled scallops, oysters. Stalls start closing around 12:00–13:00. Tokyo's best breakfast. Short subway ride from most central hotels.
~09:30
Anime
Akihabara — Morning Blitz
Multi-floor Mandarake, Animate, Radio Kaikan, Super Potato retro games, capsule machines. Give it 1.5 hrs then head for PokéPark — you can always return tonight!
~11:00
Transit
Akihabara → Keio Yomiuriland Station
JR Chuo Line to Shinjuku (~25 min), then Keio Line to Keio Yomiuriland Station (~20 min), then the Sky Shuttle aerial tram (5–10 min) to the park entrance. Total ~1 hour from Akihabara.
~12:00
PokéPark
🎮 PokéPark KANTO — Yomiuriland
The world's first permanent outdoor Pokémon attraction — opened February 2026! Two zones: Pokémon Forest (a 500m nature trail where 600+ Pokémon appear in recreated wild habitats) and Sedge Town (live shows, parades, Pokémon Center, and the exclusive Pokémon Daisuki Shop). Open the app immediately upon entering to grab your numbered Daisuki Shop slot — they fill up fast. Pikachu and Eevee ice bars are a must. 🍦 Budget 3–4 hours.
~16:00
Transit
PokéPark → Akihabara (Return)
Head back for the Maid Café experience. @Home Cafe or Maidreamin are the most popular. ¥1,500–2,500 per person. Book a slot in advance, especially on weekends.
~17:00
Anime
Maid Café Experience
Staff stay in character the entire time — no photos without permission. Uniquely Akihabara. A fun cap to a massive Pokémon-and-anime day.
~19:30
Food
Dinner: Yakiniku or Ramen
Good yakiniku spots around Akihabara and Ueno. Budget ¥2,000–3,500 per person. Early night — DisneySea tomorrow starts at 09:00!

04
Day Trip
Lake Kawaguchiko + Mt. Fuji Ropeway
Leave Tokyo early · Return evening
⚠️ Weather check: Mt. Fuji is often clouded over, especially in summer. September–October gives you the best chance of a clear view. Check the forecast the night before. If it's cloudy, the lake is still beautiful — but a clear Fuji is life-changing.
06:30
Transit
Shinjuku → Kawaguchiko
Take the Fujikyuko Highway Bus from Shinjuku Station Bus Terminal (~2 hrs, ¥1,800–2,000 one way). Book ahead on Keio Bus website. Alternatively, take JR to Otsuki then Fujikyuko Railway to Kawaguchiko.
~09:00
Nature
Lake Kawaguchiko
Walk the lakeside path for the best Fuji reflections. In October the maples begin turning — the view of Fuji framed by autumn colors is one of Japan's most iconic sights. Oishi Park is a great lakeside spot with wide open Fuji views.
~11:00
Nature
Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway
Take a short bus or taxi to the ropeway. Ride up Mt. Tenjo (1,075m) for a dramatic bird's-eye view of Lake Kawaguchi with Mt. Fuji behind. ¥1,000 round trip. Takes ~3 min each way. Clear mornings are best — avoid midday haze.
~13:00
Food
Lunch in Kawaguchiko
Try local hoto noodles (flat noodles in miso soup with pumpkin — a Yamanashi specialty). Several restaurants near the lake. Budget ¥1,200–1,800 per person.
~15:00
Transit
Kawaguchiko → Suidobashi (Tokyo)
Your itinerary notes "Fuji Station to Suidobashi" — take the bus back to Shinjuku (~2 hrs). Suidobashi is near Tokyo Dome area if you want to explore before heading back to the hotel.
~19:00
Food
Tsukiji Outer Market — Dinner Visit
Last Tsukiji stop! Note: most stalls close by 13:00–14:00 but some restaurants and shops stay open for dinner. Great sushi dinner options around Tsukiji in the evening.
🚅 Tomorrow: Shinkansen Tokyo → Kyoto (~2h15min, ~¥13,870). Book seat reservations in advance — especially as a group of 4!

Shinkansen Day · New City
Tokyo → Kyoto · Fushimi Inari + Kimono
~2h15m on Shinkansen · Arrive midday
~08:00
Transit
Tokyo Station → Kyoto Station
Take the Tokaido Shinkansen (Hikari or Nozomi). ~2h15min to Kyoto. Window seats on the right side going west for Fuji views on a clear morning. Buy ekiben (station bento) — a classic Shinkansen ritual.
~10:30
Transit
Check in Kyoto Hotel
Stay near Kyoto Station, Gion, or Kawaramachi for the best access. Store luggage and head out immediately — you have a full afternoon.
~11:30
Experience
Kimono Rental — Gion/Higashiyama
Rent kimono near the Higashiyama district — many shops along Sannen-zaka offer 2–4 hour rentals including hair styling (¥3,000–5,000). You'll look amazing for the shrine and temple photos that follow.
~12:30
Culture
Ninenzaka & Sannenzaka
The iconic stone-paved historic streets of Kyoto. Walk slowly, browse the ceramics shops and tea houses. Perfect for kimono photos. Connects directly up to Kiyomizu-dera.
~13:30
Temple
Kiyomizu-dera
One of Japan's most spectacular temples — a wooden stage jutting out over the hillside with sweeping views of Kyoto. Open 06:00–18:00. ¥500 entry. Go early or late afternoon to dodge peak crowds. The autumn colors in October here are exceptional.
~15:30
Shrine
Fushimi Inari Taisha
Open 24 hours, free entry. The famous thousands of vermilion torii gates. Go in late afternoon — as crowds thin and the light turns golden. You don't need to hike all the way up (full hike = 2–3 hrs roundtrip). First 30 min of the trail is spectacular enough. Wear comfortable shoes.
~19:00
Food
Dinner in Gion / Nishiki Area
Kaiseki-style set menus are Kyoto's specialty (¥3,000–6,000 at mid-range). Or try a cozy izakaya near Pontocho Alley — the narrow lantern-lit alley along the river is magical at night.

Kyoto + Nagoya Day Trip
Ghibli Park → Arashiyama → Golden Pavilion
Nagoya detour · Back to Kyoto evening
⚠️ Ghibli Park tickets are VERY hard to get. Tickets sell out months in advance via lottery on the Chunichi Web ticketing site. The park is CLOSED on Tuesdays. Also only open until 17:00. Plan this on a Wed–Mon. The Ghibli Grand Warehouse (main building) requires a separate ticket from the outdoor areas.
~07:30
Transit
Kyoto → Nagoya (Shinkansen)
~35 min on the Shinkansen (Nozomi). Then take the Higashiyama Subway Line to Fujigaoka Station and transfer to the Linimo monorail to Ghibli Park (~30 min total from Nagoya Station).
10:00
Ghibli
Ghibli Park
Howl's Moving Castle area, Witch Valley, Mononoke Village, Grand Warehouse. The park isn't a typical thrill-ride park — it's an immersive walk-through world for fans. Budget 3–4 hours. Buy all merch you want here — some items are park-exclusive. Closes 17:00.
~15:30
Transit
Nagoya → Kyoto (Shinkansen)
~35 min back. Arrive Kyoto ~16:30.
~17:00
Temple
Tenryu-ji — Arashiyama
UNESCO World Heritage Zen garden. Open 08:30–17:00 (last entry 16:50). ¥500 for garden, ¥800 for garden + interior. The reflected garden in the Sogen Pond is one of Kyoto's most serene sights. Walk through the bamboo grove just behind the temple — short but stunning.
~18:30
Shrine
Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
Open 09:00–17:00 — this is tight! Kinkakuji may need to shift to an early morning visit if timing doesn't work. ¥500 entry. The gold-leafed temple reflecting in the mirror pond is one of Japan's most photographed sights. Come early to avoid the crowds that build by 10:00.
~19:30
Food + Shopping
Nishiki Market + Dinner
Nishiki is best visited in the afternoon/early evening. Narrow 5-block covered market — try pickled vegetables, yuba (tofu skin), matcha soft serve, grilled skewers. Pokemon Center Kyoto is also nearby in the Takashimaya department store if you want to hit that.
🚅 Tomorrow: Shinkansen Kyoto → Osaka (~14 min, ~¥560 on Shinkansen or ~¥560 JR Biwako/Special Rapid). Super easy, frequent trains.

New City
Osaka — Dotonbori · Castle · Pokemon · Jump Shop
Osaka is louder, cheaper, and delicious
~09:00
Transit
Kyoto → Osaka · Check In
14 min by Shinkansen or ~30 min by JR Special Rapid (¥560). Stay near Namba or Shinsaibashi for the best access to Dotonbori and shopping.
~10:30
History
Osaka Castle
Open 09:00–17:00. ¥600 museum entry. The castle grounds are free and beautiful. Climb the main tower for panoramic city views. October is a great time — the surrounding park turns gold with ginkgo trees.
~13:00
Food
Kuromon Ichiba Market
Osaka's kitchen. Fresh wagyu skewers, giant crab legs, fugu (blowfish), takoyaki, oysters, sea urchin. Come hungry. Budget ¥2,000–3,500 for a full market crawl lunch. Open until 18:00.
~15:00
Shopping
Pokemon Center Osaka DX + Jump Shop
Both are in Daimaru Shinsaibashi — 9F. Pokemon Center DX is bigger and has a cafe (reservation needed). Jump Shop is right across the hall (One Piece, JJK, Dragon Ball, Haikyuu). Budget 1.5–2 hrs and your wallet. 😅
~18:00
Nightlife
Dotonbori — Evening + Dinner
The neon-lit canal district comes alive at night. The giant Glico running man, Kani Doraku crab signs, Ichiran ramen, takoyaki stalls. Eat everything. Osaka's motto is kuidaore — "eat until you drop." This is the night for it. Walk along the canal, find a karaoke bar after.

Japan Food List
Don't leave Japan without trying these
Tokyo
🐟
Tsukiji Breakfast
Toro sashimi, grilled scallops, tamagoyaki — early morning only. The freshest seafood breakfast you'll ever have.
🍣
Standing Sushi Bar
Tokyo has dozens of standing sushi counters. Fresh, fast, affordable — ¥100–300 per piece. Try Uobei Shibuya.
🍜
Ramen
Tokyo-style shoyu (soy) ramen is the classic. Ichiran for solo booths, Fuunji for tsukemen (dipping ramen).
🥚
Convenience Store Meals
7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson — onigiri, egg sandwiches, hot oden. Japanese konbini food is legitimately excellent.
Kyoto
🍱
Kaiseki
Kyoto's multi-course seasonal cuisine. Refined, beautiful, unforgettable. Budget ¥5,000–10,000 for a proper set.
🍵
Matcha Everything
Matcha ice cream, matcha latte, matcha mochi — Kyoto is the matcha capital. Try Nishiki Market for the best versions.
🫘
Yudofu (Tofu Hot Pot)
Soft silken tofu simmered in kombu broth — a Kyoto temple dish. Simple, delicate, warming for October evenings.
🐟
Nishiki Street Food
Grilled skewers, pickled vegetables, fresh mochi. A slow walk through Nishiki Market IS a meal.
Osaka
🐙
Takoyaki
Osaka's #1 street food. Octopus balls with bonito flakes and mayo. Dotonbori has the best spots — Aizuya or Kukuru.
🍳
Okonomiyaki
Savory pancake with cabbage, pork, seafood. "Osaka-style" means everything mixed together. Mizuno on Dotonbori is iconic.
🥩
Wagyu Skewers at Kuromon
Bite-sized A5 wagyu on a stick at the market. Expensive but worth every yen — ¥800–1,500 per skewer.
🍢
Kushikatsu
Deep-fried breaded skewers — the quintessential Osaka bar food. Golden rule: never double-dip the sauce.

Essential
Japan Travel Tips
Things to know before you go
💳
IC Card (Suica/Pasmo)
Get one at Narita on arrival. Load ¥5,000–10,000. Tap in/out on all trains and subways across Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka. Also works at 7-Eleven and most convenience stores.
📶
Data SIM or Pocket WiFi
Buy a data SIM at the airport (IIJmio, Mobal) or rent a pocket WiFi. Essential for navigating — Google Maps Japan works perfectly offline too if you download the region.
🚅
JR Pass (Consider It)
For 4 people doing Tokyo–Kyoto–Osaka by Shinkansen, a 7-day JR Pass (~¥50,000/person) may break even. Calculate your routes first — it's not always worth it for short trips.
💴
Cash Still King
Many smaller restaurants, shrines, and markets are cash-only. Always carry ¥5,000–10,000 on you. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards reliably.
📅
Book Tickets Early
Shibuya Sky, DisneySea, Ghibli Park, USJ Express Pass — all require advance booking. Ghibli Park sells out months ahead via lottery. Don't leave these to the last minute.
🗣️
Language
Very few people speak English outside tourist areas. Download Google Translate with Japanese (camera mode is amazing for menus). Most train stations have English signage. Locals are incredibly patient and helpful.
🎒
Coin Lockers
Every major train station has coin lockers (¥300–700/day). Use them on day trips so you don't carry luggage. IC card-operated lockers are the easiest.
🕌
Shrine Etiquette
Bow slightly when entering torii gates. Cleanse hands at the chōzuya (water basin) before approaching shrines. No flash photography inside temples. Be quiet — these are active places of worship.
🍂
October Weather
Perfect travel weather — 18–26°C in Tokyo, slightly cooler in Kyoto/Osaka. Pack layers for evenings. Early October is still warm; late October can be crisp. Rain is possible — pack a light umbrella.
🛍️
Tax-Free Shopping
Spend over ¥5,000 at participating stores and you get 10% tax back at the counter (show your passport). Department stores, electronics shops, and drug stores all participate. Big savings on cosmetics and electronics.
